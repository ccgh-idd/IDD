Understanding Dynamics

Understanding the underlying ecological and transmission dynamics is often key to the design of effective control strategies for infectious disease. The IDD lab at Charité Center for Global Health connects an interdisciplinary group of experts in mathematical modelling, statistics, clinical trials, and policy.

We work in close connection with other centres and international collaborators to increase our understanding of disease dynamics and thereby disease prevention. We also host the secretariat to Charite's IDD theme, the Berlin Infectious Disease Dynamics Expertise network (B-IDDE) and the Pneumococcal and Pneumonia INterest Group (PING)

June 2025

HUMOD Workshop

Read Abstract

Infectious disease modelling has transformed outbreak preparedness and response in stable settings, yet its application in humanitarian crises remains limited despite high disease burden and disrupted health systems. We convened the Humanitarian Modelling Dialogue (HUMOD) workshop in June 2025, bringing together 40 epidemiologists, modellers, vaccination advisors, and humanitarian practitioners to examine the role of modelling in crisis settings. Through presentations, scenario-based exercises, panel discussions, and a hackathon, participants explored where modelling can add operational value, the barriers to its use, and strategies to strengthen collaboration.

Participants identified forecasting, resource prioritisation, vaccination strategy design, and intervention evaluation as priority applications. However, four cross-cutting challenges emerged: poor data quality and access; mismatches between model complexity and available data or operational needs; difficulty communicating uncertain results in high-pressure contexts; and structural and cultural divides between academic and humanitarian communities. Proposed solutions included strengthening and stabilising data frameworks, leveraging alternative data sources, prioritising simple and adaptable models, translating probabilistic outputs into actionable metrics, and establishing a Community of Practice to coordinate efforts. Embedding modelling capacity within humanitarian structures, while sustaining academic partnerships, is essential to translate analytical advances into timely, context-sensitive, and operationally relevant decision support during crises.
